IISE Simulation and AI Case Study

Hospital Patient Flow Optimization

This project treated a hospital like an operations flow system. I modeled patient movement through emergency intake, triage, treatment, staffing constraints, and discharge to identify bottlenecks, test capacity decisions, and show how predictive AI could improve planning before real resources were changed.

Patient Flow Process

The system was modeled as a sequence of service stations with queues, constrained resources, and patient classes.

1

Emergency Intake

Patients enter the system based on forecasted arrival patterns and are assigned to a patient class.

2

Triage

The model separates patient urgency levels so high priority cases can receive faster routing.

3

Treatment

Rooms and providers are treated as constrained resources that control queue length and service speed.

4

Discharge

Length of stay forecasts affect when rooms and providers become available for the next patient.

5

Dashboard Output

Wait time, throughput, utilization, and bottleneck trends are summarized for decision making.

Arena Simulation Logic

Example of how the process logic could be structured in a discrete event simulation model.

simulation_process_logic.txt
Create Patient Entity
  Assign PatientClass using predicted acuity mix
  Assign ArrivalTime based on AI forecast distribution

Process Intake
  Seize IntakeStaff
  Delay IntakeServiceTime
  Release IntakeStaff

Decide Patient Priority
  If PatientClass == HighAcuity route to PriorityTriage
  Else route to StandardTriage

Process Treatment
  Seize Provider and TreatmentRoom
  Delay ForecastedLengthOfStay
  Release Provider and TreatmentRoom

Record Metrics
  WaitTime, QueueLength, Utilization, Throughput
